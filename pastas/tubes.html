Bucatini
 " boo-kah-tee-nee "

What: "Little holes." Bucatini looks like a thicker spaghetti but it is a fairly light pasta because it has a hole running through the center. The name comes from 'buco', meaning hole, while 'bucato' means pierced. adding '-ino' or '-ini' means small.


Where: One of the most popular pastas in Rome.


How: Best served with buttery sauces, pancetta or guanciale, and anchovies or sardines.

Penne
 " pen-ne "

What: "Quills". Also known as Mostaccioli ("Small Mustaches"), penne is a long and tube-shaped pasta. Most penne pasta is cut on the bias and contains ridges to help sauce better adhere to the pasta.
