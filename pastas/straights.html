Linguini
" ling-gwee-nee "

What: Linguini is like fettuccine and trenette, but elliptical rather than flat. It is about 4 mm wide, which is wider than spaghetti but not as wide as fettuccine. The name linguine means "little tongues" in Italian, where it is a plural of the feminine linguina. Linguine are also called 'trenette' or 'bavette.' A thinner version of linguine is called 'linguettine'.

Where:Linguine originated in Genoa and the Liguria region of Italy. Linguine alle vongole (linguine with clams) and Trenette al pesto are popular uses of this pasta.

How: Linguine are often served with seafood or pesto.

Fusilli Lunghi
" fyoo-see-lee loo ng-gee "

What: Long, thick, corkscrew shaped pasta. The name comes from the word 'fucile,' meaning rifle (as is the inside barrel of a gun). In addition to plain and whole wheat varieties, other colors can be made by mixing ingredients into the dough (beetroot, tomato, spinach, or cuttlefish ink), which also affects the flavor. Hollow fusilli are also called fusilli bucati.

Where: Camparia, Italy.

How: Fusilli is spun by pressing and rolling a small rod over the thin strips of pasta to wind them around it in a corkscrew shape. They are traditionally served with Neapolitan ragu or meat sauces.

Spinach & Artichoke Linguine

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 pound whole wheat linguine
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 shallots, diced
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 8 oz fresh spinach
  • 1 (12-oz) jar of marinated artichokes, drained and coarsely chopped
  • 1/2 c. ricotta cheese
  • 1/3 c. freshly grated parmesan cheese, plus more for topping
  • 3 tbsp mascarpone cheese

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1 Bring water to a boil and prepare pasta according to directions.
  2. 2 Heat a large skillet over medium-low heat and add olive oil and butter. Add in shallots, salt and pepper and cook for about 5 minutes, just until soft.
  3. 3 Add in spinach and toss well to coat, stirring until completely wilted, then add in garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. 4 Add in artichokes and stir, cooking for another 5 minutes so everything is warm. Stir in ricotta, mascarpone and parmesan, mixing until combined.
  5. 5 Add the drained pasta to the skillet and toss thoroughly with the spinach and artichoke mixture. Serve immediately with extra cheese!

Fusilli Lunghi with Sausage & Butternut Squash Ragu

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 package fusilli lunghi
  • 1/2 onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, roughly chopped
  • 1 small carrot, roughly chopped
  • 4 links spicy Italian sausage, casing removed and crumbled
  • Salt
  • 1 c. plus 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 butternut squash, cubed
  • freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, about 3/4 c. or to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 c. chopped parsley
  • 1/2 c. cream
  • pinch salt
  • 1/2 nutmeg, or to taste
  • 1/4 c. freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1 Add the onion, celery, carrot, pinch of salt and few tablespoons of olive oil to the food processor. Chop in food processor until almost a paste, to make a soffrito.
  2. 2 Add soffrito and a pinch of salt to a large pot and cook until translucent and just starting to brown. Add pasta water to soffrito to slow the cooking process if need be. Add crumbled sausage and cook until browned. Pour in 1 c. of white wine and bring up to a full boil for 1 minute. Pulse this mixture in the food processor until the texture is smooth (about 20 pulses).
  3. 3 Add 2 tbsp EVOO and cubed butternut squash to emptied pot. Season with salt and sauté. When squash begins to brown, add about 1/2 c. white wine and use a wooden spoon to scrape up the browned bits from bottom of the pan.
  4. 4 Drop the fusilli into the water, submerge at once, and cook for 1 minute less than the package instructions indicate.
  5. 5 Add the soffrito back into the pot with the butternut squash and stir for 4-5 minutes, until the butternut squash is tender and the raw alcohol has cooked out of the wine. If sauce becomes dry, add a ladleful of pasta water and continue to stir. Remove from heat and allow to sit until pasta is cooked.
  6. 6 To make the fonduta: In a small saucepan, bring 1/2 c. of cream to a boil and season with salt. Freshly grate 1/2 nutmeg into the cream. Remove from heat when it has thickened slightly and allow to cool for a 1 minute. Add 1/4 c. freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano.
  7. 7 When pasta is ready, place sauce back on heat and add drained pasta, stirring together with another ladleful of pasta water. Remove from heat and add 2-3 tbsp of EVOO, 1/2 c. freshly grated Parmigianno-Reggiano and freshly chopped parsley. Toss together.
  8. 8 Pour a small amount of the fonduta on the plate, then place the pasta and butternut squash on top. Grate more Parmigiano-Reggiano over the top.

Mushroom & Spinach Pappardelle Pasta with White Wine Cream Sauce

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 tbsp butter, cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 12 oz mixed mushrooms, sliced or chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 5 c. baby spinach
  • salt & black pepper
  • 1/2 c. white wine
  • 1 1/2 c. vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp flour mixed with 1 tbsp water
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper
  • 1/4 c. heavy cream
  • 1/2 c. grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 lb pappardelle pasta
  • 2 tbsp chopped basil
  • 1/4 c. pine nuts, lightly toasted
  • fresh sliced burrata

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1 Add 1 tablespoon of butter and olive oil to a large sauté pan over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook until caramelized and brown, about 5-6 minutes.
  2. 2 Add the garlic and spinach. Sprinkle with a generous amount of salt and pepper. Cook until the spinach has wilted, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. 3 Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter and stir until melted. Pour in the white wine and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil and stir in the slurry. Reduce the heat to a simmer and sprinkle in the crushed red pepper. Cook until thickened and reduced, about 10 minutes.
  4. 4 While the sauce is reducing, bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the pappardelle pasta and cook until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  5. 5 Add the heavy cream and 1/4 cup of parmesan to the sauce and cook for an additional 2 minutes or until the cheese has melted. Then, add the pappardelle pasta to the pan with the sauce and toss to combine.
  6. 6 Top the pasta with additional parmesan cheese, basil, pine nuts and fresh burrata.

Pesto Squash Noodles & Spaghetti with Cherry Tomatoes

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 c. packed fresh arugula and/or basil, tough stems removed
  • 1/4 c. sliced shelled pecans
  • 1/2 c. freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 medium garlic cloves, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 c. extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 medium zucchini, julienne
  • 1 medium yellow squash, julienne
  • 5 oz spaghetti
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Dash red pepper flakes
  • Dash salt

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1 Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cut off the tough ends of the squash, and use a peeler to slice the squash lengthwise. Stop once you get to the seeded part, then turn the squash to work on the next side.
  2. 2 Toast the pecans: In a large skillet over medium heat, toast the pecans, stirring frequently, until fragrant. About 4 - 5 minutes. Transfer the pecans to a bowl to cool.
  3. 3 To make the pesto: In a food processor, combine the arugula/basil, cooled pecans, Parmesan, garlic and salt. Pulse while drizzling in the olive oil. Stir in the lemon juice and season to taste with freshly ground black pepper.
  4. 4 Once the water is boiling, cook the pasta until al dente, according to package directions. Before draining, reserve 1/2 cup pasta cooking water. Drain pasta and return to pot.
  5. 5 Cook the tomatoes: Slice about 5 of the tomatoes into thin rounds and reserve for later. In a Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat, warm 2 tablespoons olive oil and red pepper flakes. Once the oil is shimmering, add the whole cherry tomatoes and a dash of salt. Let the tomatoes cook, stirring occasionally, until they are blistered in spots and starting to pop, about 3 minutes. Continue to cook, lightly crushing the tomatoes with the back of a big spoon or silicone spatula, until they are beginning to break down, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the sliced tomatoes.
  6. 6 To assemble the pasta: Combine the pasta and squash noodles in a serving bowl. Add most of the pesto and a couple tablespoons pasta water. Toss to combine, adding more pesto or pasta water until the noodles are sufficiently coated in pesto. Season to taste with salt and black pepper. Divide the noodles into individual bowls and top with cherry tomatoes.

Shrimp Fra Diavolo with Vermicelli

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tbsp. cooking oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 16oz. can of crushed tomatoes in thick puree
  • 1/4 tsp. dried red-pepper flakes
  • 1/4 c. water (or dry white wine)
  • 1/4 c. chopped fresh parsley
  • 3/4 tsp. salt and pepper
  • 1 lb. medium shrimp, shelled
  • 3/4 lb. vermicelli

DIRECTIONS

  1. 1 Heat the oil over moderately low heat in a large frying pan. Add the onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until translucent (~5 minutes).
  2. 2 Add the garlic and cook, stirring, 30 seconds longer. Stir in the tomatoes, red-pepper flakes, water (or wine), salt, and pepper. Reduce the heat and simmer, covered, for 10 minutes.
  3. 3 Add the shrimp and cook, covered, just until the shrimp are pink (~ 4 minutes).
  4. 4 In a large pot of boiling, salted water, cook the vermicelli until just done (~ 9 minutes). Drain the pasta and toss with the tomato sauce and shrimp.
  5. 5 Top with fresh parsley
